Frequently Asked Questions

How does stair tracking work?

Climb Up uses two technologies to track your stair climbing:

  • Apple HealthKit: Reads "Flights Climbed" data that your iPhone automatically collects from the motion sensors.
  • CoreMotion (CMPedometer): Provides real-time stair counting and step detection for immediate feedback.

Both data sources are processed entirely on your device — nothing is sent to our servers.

What health data does the app access?

Climb Up reads the following health and fitness data from HealthKit (read-only, no writing):

  • Flights Climbed
  • Step Count
  • Active Energy Burned
  • Sleep Analysis

All data access is read-only. We never modify or write to your HealthKit data.

Is my data shared with anyone?

No. Your data stays on your device. We do not:

  • Send data to external servers
  • Share data with third parties
  • Use analytics or tracking services
  • Sell or monetize your health information

You have complete control over your health data.

How do I manage health permissions?

You can control which health data Climb Up can access:

  1. Open Settings on your iPhone
  2. Go to Privacy → Health
  3. Find Climb Up in the list
  4. Toggle individual data types on or off
  5. Or tap the app name to revoke all permissions

You can change these permissions at any time.
