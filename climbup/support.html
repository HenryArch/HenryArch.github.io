Frequently Asked Questions

How does floor tracking work?

ClimbUp uses your iPhone's built-in barometric altimeter and Apple HealthKit to automatically count the floors you climb throughout the day. No manual input is required — just keep your iPhone with you.

Each floor is approximately 3 meters (10 feet) of elevation gain, as defined by Apple Health.

What health data does the app access?

ClimbUp reads the following health data from HealthKit (read-only, no writing):

  • Flights Climbed
  • Step Count
  • Active Energy Burned
  • Sleep Analysis

All data access is read-only. We never modify or write to your HealthKit data.

Is my data shared with anyone?

No. Your data stays on your device. We do not:

  • Send data to external servers
  • Share data with third parties
  • Use analytics or tracking services
  • Sell or monetize your health information

You have complete control over your health data.

How do I manage health permissions?

You can control which health data ClimbUp can access:

  1. Open Settings on your iPhone
  2. Go to Privacy → Health
  3. Find ClimbUp in the list
  4. Toggle individual data types on or off
  5. Or tap the app name to revoke all permissions

You can change these permissions at any time.

What is Live Climbing Mode?

Live Climbing Mode is a real-time tracking feature designed for dedicated stair climbing sessions. When you start a session, ClimbUp tracks your floors climbed, steps, calories burned, and elapsed time in real-time.

Perfect for stairwell workouts, hiking, or any activity where you want to see your progress as it happens.

Does it work with Apple Watch?

Yes! ClimbUp includes a dedicated Apple Watch app with:

  • Climbing dashboard with today's floor count
  • Goal progress ring
  • Steps and calories at a glance
  • Weekly trend chart

The Apple Watch app works independently — no need to pull out your iPhone to check your progress.

Is ClimbUp a medical device?

No. ClimbUp is a wellness and fitness tracking tool, not a medical device. It does not provide medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment. Information displayed is for general wellness purposes only.

If you have health concerns, please consult a healthcare professional.
